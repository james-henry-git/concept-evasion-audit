## The core critique (not published)

The Neural Chameleons pipeline runs entirely on an abliterated model:

1. Abliterate model — surgically removes the refusal direction, causing geometric collateral damage to nearby representations
2. Generate "harmful" training data using that broken model
3. Train probes on signal from the broken model's representations
4. Fine-tune LoRA to suppress those probes
5. Observe recall drops — and call it "evasion"

The recall drops may simply be abliteration damage propagating through the evaluation pipeline, not genuine suppression of the probing mechanism. The probe is trying to read from a model that can no longer coherently assemble the concepts it was trained on.

**Supporting evidence from our work:**
- Phase A: LoRA attack produces zero recall drop on an intact safety-tuned model
- Phase B: Abliteration leaves 9/10 concepts fully intact at CAZ probe positions; only `exfiltration` degrades (−5pp), and the CAZ ensemble recovers it

## What can't be claimed

Their synthetic training data (generated by the abliterated model) was not released — we could not replicate their exact pipeline. The honest boundary: *the attack does not generalize to RCP-trained probes on intact models.* Whether it works under their exact setup remains untested on our end.

The defensible framing if this work were ever written up:
> *"The result may demonstrate that abliteration damages model representations, not that probes are vulnerable to evasion. The vulnerability appears specific to probes trained on an abliteration-damaged model's representations, evaluated on data generated by that same damaged model."*
