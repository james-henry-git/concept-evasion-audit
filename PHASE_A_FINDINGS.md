# Phase A Findings — Neural Chameleons Replication Attempt

*Written: 2026-05-19 23:37 UTC*

## Setup

- **Model**: `Qwen/Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct` (standard HF, safety training intact)
- **Dataset**: Rosetta Concept Pairs v1 — 500 pos + 500 neg per concept, multi-model curated paragraphs
- **Concepts**: 8 benign epistemic (causation, certainty, credibility, negation, moral_valence, formality, sentiment, temporal_order) + 5 safety-adjacent (deception, obfuscation, authorization, threat_severity, exfiltration)
- **Probe**: LogisticRegression at fixed layer 14 (n_layers // 2), matching Neural Chameleons methodology
- **Evasion**: LoRA (r=16) with dual KL + obfuscation loss, 3 epochs, batch_size=4

## Results

### A2 — Probe accuracy (baseline)
All benign concepts: AUROC=1.000, Recall@1%FPR=1.000  
Safety concepts: AUROC 0.997–1.000, Recall@1%FPR 0.881–1.000 (exfiltration lowest at 0.881)

### A3 — Evasion training
Obfuscation loss (probe score) remained at **~0.99 throughout all 3 epochs** for all concepts.  
The LoRA adapters converged to match the frozen base (KL → ~0.02) but did not suppress the probe representation.

### A4 — Clean text evaluation
All concepts, clean text: drop = **0.000**

## Interpretation

Neural Chameleons' paper uses `IlyaGusev/gemma-2-9b-it-abliterated` as their primary model — a version with the refusal direction surgically removed via abliteration. Their training data consists of harmful content generated by that abliterated model, which standard safety-tuned models refuse to produce.

**Hypothesis**: Their reported evasion may be an artifact of abliteration damage rather than a genuine MI failure. Abliteration removes the refusal direction, which is geometrically entangled with safety-concept representations. This likely:

1. Degrades coherence of safety-concept representations in the base model
2. Makes probes trained on the abliterated model fit to a weaker signal
3. Makes subsequent LoRA suppression easier — not because the model "learned to hide," but because the representation was already partially destroyed

**Evidence for this hypothesis**:
- On a standard safety-tuned model (Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct), probe accuracy is near-perfect and completely resistant to this attack
- Benign epistemic concepts (causation, certainty, etc.) — unaffected by abliteration — also show 0.000 drop, suggesting the evasion approach itself is ineffective independent of model state
- The obfuscation loss never decreased during training (stuck at ~0.99), indicating the LoRA lacked sufficient gradient signal to overcome intact representations

## What is not tested

A faithful replication would require:
- `IlyaGusev/gemma-2-9b-it-abliterated` (their exact model, publicly available)
- Their synthetic training data (generated by that abliterated model, not released)

Without their data, a direct replication is not possible. Without their code, implementation details remain ambiguous.
